Undergraduate Researcher

North Dakota State University: Fargo, ND

June 2021 – August 2021

  • During my internship at the NDSU Cancer Research Center under Dr. Kalpana Katti, I worked on evaluating Metarrestin, a potential therapy for advanced bone-metastasized prostate cancer.
  • I performed in vitro drug trials, culturing prostate cancer and mesenchymal stem cells, and analyzed cell viability post-treatment using spectrophotometry.
  • My findings were promising! Metarrestin had an IC50 of 52.5 μM against prostate cancer cells, with minimal effects on mesenchymal stem cells, meaning it has potential as a targeted therapy for bone metastasized prostate cancer.
  • This experience not only gave me valuable training in cancer drug development but also highlighted how foundational research can have real-world applications.
Cell culture Spectrophotometry Drug Efficacy Analysis Cancer Research Translational Research
